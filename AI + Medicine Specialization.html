AI + Medicine Specialization

Co-designing the future of health technology education.

Who This Path Is For

Educators

Science and health teachers, biology instructors, wellness coordinators, and STEM educators who want to bring real-world health and biomedical contexts into their classrooms.

Domain Professionals

Biomedical engineers, healthcare data scientists, and medical researchers seeking to translate their expertise into education — mentoring future-ready AI learners.

Together, these two groups form the dual-mentor ecosystem of STEM AI Studio — where teachers bring pedagogy, and professionals bring lived domain insight.

What You’ll Learn (Learning Goals)

  • Translate core CS/AI ideas into health contexts (classification, triage, and pattern detection).
  • Teach human-centered AI ethics (privacy, consent, bias, transparency).
  • Design age-appropriate simulations (e.g., symptom triage, image cues, wellness dashboards).
  • Collaborate with a Subject Matter Expert (SME) or mentor reviewer to ensure accuracy and realism.

How This Path Was Designed

Developed through consultation with medical educators and health-data researchers to align classroom projects with authentic healthcare challenges.

This specialization is not about diagnosing illness — it’s about helping students understand how AI supports human decision-making in medicine and wellness responsibly.

Core Learning Experience — Case-Clinics

Each cohort participates in three short “Case-Clinic” sessions (30–45 min each) led by mentor-facilitated teams. These sessions ground theory in realistic, classroom-ready examples.

Case-Clinic Focus Sample Questions
1. AI in Triage & Response How chatbots and classifiers support first-line care safely. “When does AI advice end and human care begin?”
2. Data & Bias in Wearables Fairness and accuracy of health-monitoring AI. “What makes a dataset representative enough for schools?”
3. Public-Health Dashboards Communicating data ethically and effectively. “How can visualizations inform without alarming?”

After each clinic, you’ll post a 3-sentence reflection and a short classroom-translation idea.

Sample Capstones

  • 1️⃣ AI Triage Chatbot for School Nurses — intent classification + “seek adult help” guardrails.
  • 2️⃣ Predicting Asthma Triggers with Wearables — synthetic time-series + risk-alert poster.
  • 3️⃣ Early-Warning Flu Trends — anonymized data → awareness campaign dashboard.
  • 4️⃣ Healthy Habits Coach — behavior prompts, reflection journal, bias check on suggestions.

Starter Mini-Labs

  • Teachable Machine: classify healthy vs unsafe lunch items; ethics of labeling.
  • Datawrapper: symptom-trend dashboard; discuss data limits.
  • Canva: privacy-consent poster design.

Tool Stack

Core:

Scratch • Teachable Machine • Canva • Datawrapper • ChatGPT / Gemini

Optional:

BioRender • Google Colab (lite demos) • Kaggle (browse-only)

Data, Privacy & Safety

  • Only synthetic or public, de-identified data.
  • No personal health data or biometric uploads.
  • Provide content warnings; refer students to trusted adults for real health issues.

Portfolio Evidence

  • 1–2 min demo video
  • Ethics note (200–300 words)
  • Screenshot(s) of poster or dashboard

Outcome

By the end of this specialization, educators and domain professionals will be able to co-design and lead AI-in-health projects that balance innovation with empathy — preparing learners to see technology as a partner in human wellbeing.
