STEM AI Studio Code of Conduct

As a participant in the Certified STEM AI Architect™ Program, I agree to uphold the following standards of professionalism, respect, and integrity throughout my learning journey.

1. Professionalism & Respect

  • Treat all learners, mentors, and guest speakers with courtesy, empathy, and cultural sensitivity.
  • Communicate constructively in all discussions, whether written, verbal, or online.
  • Respect diverse perspectives — disagreement is welcome, disrespect is not.

2. Academic Integrity

  • Submit only my own original work.
  • Credit all collaborators, sources, and AI-generated content used in projects.
  • Avoid plagiarism, data falsification, or reusing another learner’s project without acknowledgment.

3. Privacy & Digital Safety

  • Use only public or school-safe data and images.
  • Do not share screenshots, recordings, or personal information from sessions without consent.
  • Follow applicable data-protection policies (e.g., FERPA, GDPR).

4. Ethical Technology Use

  • Use AI tools responsibly and only for approved educational purposes.
  • Avoid using AI systems to generate harmful, biased, or inappropriate content.
  • Be transparent about AI’s role in any output I present.

5. Collaboration & Community

  • Offer constructive feedback and celebrate peer achievements.
  • Maintain a supportive, inclusive environment across all specialization paths.
  • Represent STEM AI Studio with professionalism when sharing public project work.

STEM AI Studio — AI Ethics Pledge

As a Certified STEM AI Architect™ candidate, I recognize that artificial intelligence carries both extraordinary potential and profound responsibility. I therefore pledge to approach all learning, teaching, and project creation with integrity, transparency, and human empathy.

1. Purpose & Responsibility

  • I will use AI as a tool to enhance human creativity, learning, and well-being, not to replace or diminish it.
  • I will prioritize ethical, inclusive, and learner-safe applications of AI in education.

2. Fairness & Bias Awareness

  • I will actively identify and reduce bias in data, prompts, and outputs.
  • I will design lessons that help students understand how AI systems can reflect or amplify bias — and how to question it critically.

3. Transparency & Attribution

  • I will disclose when and how AI tools are used in my work.
  • I will credit original human and AI sources appropriately.

4. Privacy & Safety

  • I will protect student data, images, and voices from unauthorized use.
  • I will comply with school or organizational data-protection policies and ensure my projects respect privacy and consent.

5. Human-Centered Design

  • I will keep human judgment, empathy, and ethics at the core of all AI-driven projects.
  • I will teach others to view AI as a partner for creative problem-solving, not a substitute for human values or decision-making.
